Abstract

Sentiment analysis offers many benefits for businesses, governments, and consumers in a digital age where data is exploding. According to Google, India currently has 234 million Indian Language users online, which is about 52% of the population and is compared with only 175 million English web users. It is estimated that by 2021 there will be 536 million Indians who use regional languages when they are online. As Marathi is spoken across much of the Indian subcontinent, it is envisaged that there will be significant levels of contribution by its users to defining India's internet volume.

Sentiment analysis or opinion mining is one of the major task of NLP. Sentiment analysis has gain much attention in recent years. In this project,we aim to tackle the problems of sentiment polarity categorization,which is one of the fundamental problems of sentiment analysis. A general process for sentiment polarity categorization is proposed with detailed process description.

The project shows how to overcome difficulties related with analyzing text in Marathi. The system proposed in this detects sentiments in text written in the Marathi language. It employs sentiment analysis to gain the required functionality. In this system, a corpus-based approach is proposed, i.e. the creation of a diverse up to date corpus of Marathi keywords, along with their individual polarities, with respect to the Word Net, which is consider as a corpus. The algorithm is used to calculate the cumulative polarity of the text and rank the sentence as positive, negative or neutral on a set scale standard.
